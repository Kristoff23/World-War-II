World War II

The Global Conflict that Changed History (1939–1945)

Overview Timeline Aftermath About

Overview

World War II (1939–1945) was a global war involving over 30 countries and resulting in more than 60 million deaths. It reshaped global power, destroyed empires, and led to the rise of new superpowers.

Timeline of Major Events

1939 – Invasion of Poland

Germany invades Poland, beginning the war. Britain and France declare war on Germany shortly after.

1940 – Battle of Britain

Britain resists heavy German air raids, marking the first major defeat of Hitler’s military forces.

1941 – Attack on Pearl Harbor

Japan attacks the U.S. naval base in Hawaii, drawing the United States into the war.

1942–43 – Battle of Stalingrad

The Soviet Union halts German advance, a major turning point on the Eastern Front.

1944 – D-Day Invasion

Allied troops land in Normandy, France, beginning the liberation of Europe from Nazi control.

1945 – End of the War

Germany surrenders in May. Atomic bombs dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki lead to Japan’s surrender.

Aftermath

The war ended in 1945 with over 60 million casualties. The United Nations was formed, colonial empires began to fall, and the Cold War between the U.S. and Soviet Union began.
